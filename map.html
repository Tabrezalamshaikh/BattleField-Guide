Erangal

Size and Theme: It is a large, 8x8 km map characterized by its lush, temperate, and forested environment, set on a fictional Russian island.

1. Terrain Diversity: The map offers a great variety of terrain, including large open fields, rolling hills, dense forests, and numerous urban areas.

2. Key Locations: Several highly contested and popular drop zones are visible, such as Pochinki, the School, Rozhok, and the Sosnovka Military Base.

3. Urban Hubs: Pochinki is centrally located and infamous for early-game close-quarters combat (CQC) due to its dense cluster of buildings.

4. High-Loot Zones: The Military Base (on the separate southern island) is a high-risk, high-reward area, known for spawning the best loot and armor.

5. Bridges and Strategy: Access to the Military Base is restricted by two main bridges, which are common choke points and critical for end-game rotation strategies.

6. Geographical Features: The map features a large mainland and a smaller Sosnovka Island in the south, separated by a sea channel.

7. Centrality: Locations like Rozhok and the School are near the map's center, making them strategically important as the safe zone often closes around them.

8. Vehicle Importance: Due to the 8x8 km size, vehicles are essential for crossing long distances and escaping the blue zone.

9. Coastal Areas: Towns like Georgopol and Novorepnoye are on the coast, often providing access to boats for rotation.

10. Gameplay Mix: Erangel supports all styles of play, from aggressive urban fighting to tactical sniping across open fields.

11.Iconic Landmarks: Other notable points include Gartach and the scattered settlements of Sosnovka (on the main island).

12. Overall Vibe: The map has a gritty, post-Soviet atmosphere with abandoned facilities and barracks, reflecting its fictional backstory.

Miramar.

1. Setting and Scale: It is a large, 8x8 km map, defined by its desert theme, featuring brown, sandy terrain, canyons, and rocky hills.

2. Open Terrain: The map is characterized by much more open ground compared to Erangel, which favors long-range engagements and sniping.

3. Key Locations: Major, densely packed urban centers include Los Leones (the largest city), El Pozo, and Pecado, which are known for high-risk, high-reward drops.

4. Unique Structures: The map features many multi-story buildings, warehouses, and the iconic Casino and Boxing Arena in Pecado.

5. Strategic Challenge: The open terrain makes rotation difficult, as players are often exposed and vulnerable to enemy fire from high ground.

6. Topography: The map is very hilly and mountainous, especially in the central and western regions, providing excellent vantage points for snipers.

7. Vehicle Necessity: Vehicles are absolutely critical for survival and timely rotations due to the map's sheer size and the lack of cover in open areas.

8. Naming Convention: Unlike the previous maps, Miramar uses Spanish-themed names for its locations, such as Los Hones and El Pozo.

9. Loot Distribution: Loot is heavily concentrated within the major cities, leading to chaotic early-game fights.

10. Coastal Areas: Coastal towns like Noverpnoye and the port areas offer alternative ways to move around the map.

11. Tactical Considerations: The lack of lush foliage means camouflage is minimal, forcing players to rely on terrain elevation and structures for cover.

12. Gameplay Focus: Miramar shifts the game's focus towards strategic positioning and vehicle management rather than constant close-quarters combat.

Erangel Remastered.

1. Theme: It retains the lush, green, temperate island theme, similar to the original Erangel, with dense forests and grassy fields.

2. Scale: The map maintains the large 8x8 km scale, evident from the scale bar at the bottom left, requiring significant travel time.

3. Hybrid Locations: It strategically mixes classic Erangel locations like School, Rozhok, Military Base, and Georgopol with areas named after other maps, such as Sanshok and El Pozo.

4. New Hot Drop: A notable addition is Paverdise Resort in the west, suggesting a high-loot, high-engagement area, potentially replacing or updating an older location.

5. Central Features: The School and Rozhok remain centrally located, making them critical for early-game action and subsequent zone rotations.

6. Southern Island: The separate southern island, featuring Sosnovska and the Military Base, is still a major high-tier loot zone.

7. Choke Points: The bridges connecting the main island to the Military Base remain the primary choke points for mid-to-late-game strategy.

8. Terrain: The terrain is varied, encompassing flat plains near Saanovska, mountainous areas near El Pozo, and coastal regions like Gartach.

9. Waterways: A river system flows through the center, adding tactical elements for crossing and providing limited cover.

10. Visual Update: The map generally has a brighter and more detailed visual style than the very first iteration of Erangel.

11. Loot & Action: The inclusion of multiple dense urban areas ensures a wide distribution of loot and continuous combat opportunities.

12. Gameplay Impact: The changes likely aim to refresh the gameplay experience, offering veteran players new drop strategies while keeping the classic Erangel feel.
